Name:
	Braised Lamb Shanks with White Bean Purée

Yield:
	Servings 4

Ingredients:
	4 large or 8 small Australian lamb shanks
	2 tablespoons olive oil
	1 large onion, peeled and roughly chopped
	2 medium carrots, peeled and roughly diced
	2 celery sticks, washed and roughly chopped
	4 cloves garlic, crushed
	2 ½ pints lamb or beef stock
	3 bay leaves, dried
	1 pound dried white beans that have been soaked in water overnight
	14 ounce canned tomatoes

Directions:
	Seal the lamb shanks by pan frying in a dry pan, or the casserole dish itself, to lightly brown.
	In a large casserole, heat the oil and brown the onion, carrots, celery and garlic. Lay the shanks in the pan, pour in the stock and allow to come to a simmer. Add the bay leaves.
	Tip the beans into a muslin cloth and tie to secure. Add the beans to the shanks and cover.
	Cook in the oven at 350°F for 1 hour. Check to see that the beans are cooked. When the beans are done, remove them  from the shanks, along with 1 cup of the cooking liquid. Add the tomatoes to the shanks and continue cooking for up to 1 hour more or until the meat is tender and coming away from the bone.
	Puree the beans in a food processor with enough of the cooking liquid to make a paste; season with salt and white pepper and store in a warm place or in a microwave proof container.
	When the shanks are ready, check seasoning and remove the bay leaves; spoon some of the reheated or hot bean puree into the centre of the plates and put a shank/s on top. Ladle some of the cooking juices around and over the shanks and serve cooked green vegetables or a ratatouille.
